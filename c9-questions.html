    Asking Good Questions

    I just read Jon Skeet’s post on asking questions on stack overflow. He outlines the importance of using the title to frame the question and of providing context to help someone understand the problem.

    When confused it can be difficult to explain exactly where you got lost. That’s part of the problem. I personally can have the tendency to be vague out of frustration or to overload the person I am asking with information. Jon Skeet outlines the importance of giving context in a controlled thoughtful way and to phrase your question in a straight forward way that broadly contains your intent without getting lost in details right away.

    A strategy I often use is to include my best guess along with my question. I find that this can help to elucidate my thought process to someone and help them figure out where it went wrong.

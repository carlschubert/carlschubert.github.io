    Thinking and Tests

    After taking an online thinking style test at I generated the graph above. According to the graph I am about equal parts abstract sequential and concrete random. About two weeks ago I took this test scored quite differently. I have some reason to doubt the test-retest reliability of this fifteen question internet quiz perhaps something can be gleaned from it by just reading though the learning types. I read though the descriptions of various thinking styles and I don't think I am concrete random. I am not a huge experimenter. I usually have a clear idea in my head before I start something and I love to iterate. I do identify more with abstract sequential. I love breaking down problem and really thinking them though before starting to really work on them. I also struggle when I am not given enough time to learn something new or given lots of regulations on how I should accomplish a task. I tend to be very comprehensive and I like to do things my own way.

    I think reading these list of rather discrete and defined characteristic can be interesting but I think personally I believe people are more varied and plastic than could ever be inferred from a multiple choice test. In fact all testing of this kind is inherently flawed. What is being measured is very secondary. As a society we love data collection but often donâ€™t think critically about what we are actually able to infer from what we have gathered. I tend prefer the reading we have done as part of devboot camp on the growth vs fixed mindset. A Growth-minded person acknowledges their own limitations and then tries to work mitigate and develop their weakness. A Fixed-minded person believes their capabilities are immutable and must simply be accepted.

    Having reflected on this, I can still acknowledge that I will not always have time to learn at my own pace. Sometimes done is better than perfect. Having this blog is not easy for me but I think it is helping me become more structured and disciplined.
